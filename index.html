This is a platform for students to share their views and opinions regarding professional skills. It is a window into the world of IEEE and trending subjects in the present day perspective. Through this newsletter we intend to bring into light the events conducted by our division.

From the Perspective of a Branch Counselor

IEEE Student Branch, VBIT was inaugurated in Dec 2006, with an initiation taken by our former Principal Dr. G. Ramprasad. I got an opportunity to work as a faculty mentor and faculty co-ordinator under the esteemed guidance of the Branch counsellors, Dr. P. S. Subramanyam (Prof. in Dept of EEE) and Dr. C. R. N. Sarma (Director, Student Affairs) which really helped me to conduct/co-ordinate the technical activities.


Later on, I was nominated as a Branch Counselor by Dr. Jayant Kulkarni (Vice Principal) from 2013 onwards and with his great support and guidance, we could conduct so many technical activities.

I feel proud to say that the events organized by IEEE-VBIT SB cater not only to the inquisitive nature of our students, but also instil a scientific temperament in them and help them to become well rounded professionals.

As a Branch Counselor, I got an opportunity to interact with students belonging to different departments and their technical capabilities have given me great pleasure and happiness.

During the last nine years, the IEEE VBIT SB in association with different chapters like COMSOC, WIE Affinity Group, Computer Society, Power & Energy Society has organized various events which made us to win many laurels at both Section & Region levels (Recent one being, R10 Exemplary SB Award).


In this academic year, in the month of August IEEE VBIT SB has organised “IEEE Week” in association with all student chapters, where students demonstrated their skills and showcased the team spirit. All the activities organised at IEEE VBIT SB provided an excellent platform to interact with the corporate world and creating invaluable bonds with corporate professionals.

I have observed that greenhorns in I year who involved in the organizational activities helped them to gain invaluable experience over a period of time and empowered themselves to take larger responsibilities which helps them to succeed in their professional career.


I would like to wish my students, all the best in their future endeavors and I am quite confident that IEEE VBIT SB reaches to greater heights with their abilities.


My heartfelt thanks to the management and Principal for their continuous support.

~Mrs.Ch.Suneetha


THE KNACK FOR A HEADWAY- “AVISHKAR”

It is quite a well-known fact that, in this present scenario of growing competition, soft-skills and presentation capabilities play a vital role in an individual’s success. To encourage and cultivate such qualities, especially, in the engineering grads, who will have a burning competitive role to play in the days to come, the culture of presenting technical papers has been introduced in the form of technical fests etc. But, as it is observed, the freshmen often hesitate to participate in such activities because of their lack of exposure, confidence and the phobia of a new environment.

Hence freshmen, most often are devoid of such chances to expose their camouflaged talents and this is where AVISHKAR steps-in and clears everything off focusing keenly on this aspect alone.

About
‘AVISHKAR – Engineering Inventions’ is a technical competition introduced by the Professional society, IEEE Student Branch of Vignana Bharathi Institute of Technology (VBIT), Ghatkesar.

This activity is the first of its kind which is basically a platform, especially for the freshmen, to explore their inner talents and develop themselves in the technical viewpoint.

• AVISHKAR was first conducted in December 2008 and later, in 2009, it was made a statewide event where students from various colleges present their presentations for which dignitaries from the IEEE Hyderabad Section would be present. Since then, AVISHKAR is being conducted in INTRA level & INTER level.
• AVISHKAR also encourages the second year undergraduates to know more about their managerial skills and how it is to work in a team under the guidance of their seniors and will get to know the work flow a professional organization.
• It offers the freshmen a unique opportunity to shed their coyness and put forward their talents and ideas before a known audience.

This event is a unique way of promoting the practical aspects of engineering course that can be put to best use if adopted by all colleges.

The event predominantly takes place in the first month of a new year for a period of two to three days, depending upon the number of participants, in the college premises itself. The participants give their presentations, on technical or non-technical topics, in front of a panel of judges who will both scrutinize them as judges and also guide them as mentors. Based on their performances, they’re respectively awarded and certified under IEEE, which promises to be helpful in the later stages of their career and a credit to their resume/CV.

Thus, making AVISHKAR a learning process resulting in refined, confident and professional individuals.

Achievements of Avishkar

AVISHKAR has received a prestigious award named ‘Darrel Chong Student Activity Award’ across the whole Asia – Pacific for its unique, innovative and one-of-a-kind design of the event. It is a widely acclaimed recognition which has uplifted the standard of the Student Branch.

AVISHKAR was published not only in various college newsletters, but also reached wide acclaim and publicity by its publications in national daily newspapers.


IEEE VBIT SB has been successful in conducting this event and helping out the freshmen, in every way possible, since its inception and aims to continue to do the same in the years to come.

Gist on the events

CODEX 2015

A quiz on C programming language, CODEX has been conducted by the members of the Computer Society Chapter of IEEE – VBIT SB on 16th December, 2015. The main motto of the event was to foster technical comprehension about the computing field amongst students. Forty students took part in this event, which comprised of two rounds that assessed their skills and programming prowess. The event received admirable response from the students as well as the faculty and the winner was awarded with a cash prize.






ELOQUENCE ’15

Eloquence ’15, an event on the theme of ‘Women Empowerment’ has been conducted by the WIE Affinity Group of IEEE – VBIT SB on 16th October, 2015. The event proved to be laudable with 60 participants with a minimal registration fee. The event consisted of three sessions: the first was a JAM (Just a Minute) session, based on amusing and fun inducing topics; the second was an Online Survey in which students from other states have also participated in and finally a debate on the current safety scenario of women. This event paved a platform for the students to express themselves on sensitive and predominant subjects prevailing in the society. The motto and objectives of the event were met as the participants expressed a positive feedback.



ENGINEERS’ DAY

IEEE – VBIT SB had the privilege to commemorate the legendary scholar and pioneer of Indian engineering, Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya. As a token of gratitude to the service that he has done for the nation, his birthday i.e. the 15th of September is celebrated as Engineers’ Day. In his remembrance, his life history and achievements have been showcased to the students through a Power Point presentation. The main motto of this event was to educate students about the significance of engineers and the role they play in society. The faculty members were complaisant about the same and explained to students the selfless attitude an engineer must imbibe for the betterment of human society.





INDUSTRIAL VISIT

The Electronics and Communication Department in collaboration with the Communication Society (ComSoc) chapter of IEEE – VBIT SB organised an Industrial visit on 12th August, 2015 for the final year students. The event intended on providing the students with real time experience on practical details of the industry which are not present in their academic curriculum. Almost ninety six students escorted by the faculty coordinators have visited the BSNL office located in Gachibowli. This event proved to be highly beneficial to the students, who will step into the industry to uncover their technical competence.



John Bardeen

The most revolutionary discovery of our era is undoubtedly the transistor. All the electronic devices that are a part of our daily life, be it a computer or a mobile phone, are the fruits of the invention of the transistor. It is the building block of electronic technology and humanity is indebted to its inventor John Bardeen.


Synonymous to the world as the Father of Transistor and BCS theory, John Bardeen is the only person who has won the prestigious Nobel Prize twice in the field of Physics in the years 1956 and 1972 respectively. Born on 23rd May, 1908 he became the pioneer who allowed the Information age to occur. He also invented the Theory of Superconductivity along with his counterparts.


He established his career in research at Bell Laboratories along with William Shockley and Walter Brattain. On December 23, 1947 he along with Brattain succeeded in creating a point- contact transistor, which was the beginning of his tryst with inventions. He later went on to propose the Theory of Superconductivity or the BCS theory with the help of his mates Leon Cooper and John Robert Schrieffer, after whose initials it was named. He is only the fourth person in mankind's history to have won the Nobel Prize twice. He is also the recipient of the IEEE Medal of Honor for his legendary contributions. January 30, 1991 was a day of despair to the world as we lost this acclaimed personality.



It has been IEEE's undying tradition to congratulate and felicitate eminent dignitaries who have a share in the development of Science and Technology. Following this noble custom IEEE - VBIT SB commemorates the great scientist John Bardeen on his birth anniversary as well on the day of his demise. We at IEEE - VBIT SB find it an opportunity to spread the greatness and spirit of such great souls.

SUCCESSFUL ENGINEER IN THE MAKING

There seem to be more writings, books, ideas, and lectures on success than successful people are. It’s a sad story that, people often talk about success and its secrets and fail to be successful. Isn’t that fascinating to advise others on how to be successful and not be able to practice it in our own lives? Often times, we find ourselves on the same shore. We give more ideas and answers to the problem, but we fail to solve it ourselves. This article focuses on how to be successful in our field – Engineering.

Competition is only getting increasing, day by day. With our 10-year olds giving lessons to parents on how to use apps or their use in smartphones, one could only start to ponder over the pace with which the coming generations could spurt in the field of technology. As an achieving engineers, it is wise to be aware of the fact that, somewhere down the line, we have to manage some brilliant brains brimming with astute Intelligence Quotient. We can now imagine how quick and precise should we have to work in order to have a sustainable future. How much eager do we have to be towards learning? For a bright future, learning at the right time is imperative. It is only at the college, we learn the in-depth concepts of everything which plays a big role in the embellishment of our future. Let me get into the mainstream.


Importantly, we need to make sure that our dreams don’t fade away. Certainly, there are some personalities in the world by whom we get inspired. Perhaps, Satya Nadella, Sundar Pichai or Dr. Kalam, who had humble beginnings and reached greater heights in their domains by being determined and having a different attitude in life or having a different go at the problem faced. By tracking their lives and their way of living will help us keep our dreams alive and kicking. Secondly, our learning should be more practical. Theoretical knowledge alone doesn’t help us, but applying it and working out on it in our labs also is crucial. Hence, we need to think about projects and researches in a field which we are comfortable at. Networking also sometimes connects us to new people with innovative or like-minded people which is a good positive.

Playing a team sport is also highly appreciable as we silently learn the benefit of teamwork and the leadership skills. It allows us to enjoy the game, improves our health, and also gives us valuable lessons that a teacher couldn’t teach. In this course, we also find our negatives. Without losing our attitude, converting our negatives to positives is a good sign of success. Designing and delivering our design also gives more knowledge and builds a scope for learning and mastering the subject. Holidays are sure to be there, while in college; by spending those productively by learning one or two additional subjects would only do well for our career. Lastly, maintaining healthy relationships with everyone is a tangible perk. Right from our classmates to our College Board directors, let’s retain our relationship strong, sharing our project ideas, research approaches, purviews of technology etc.

Above all, thou shalt not give up. Gird up your loins and battle it out. You don’t have to be a pro to build a successful product. Amateurs started Google and Apple. Professionals built the Titanic.
